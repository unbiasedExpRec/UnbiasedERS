Causally Attentive Collaborative Filtering

Anonymous Author  

1. Abstract

Attention-based recommender models hold the promise of improving performance by learning to discriminate different user/item feature importances. However, due to the existence of the latent confounders, the correlations captured by the attention mechanisms may fail to reflect the true influence of the features on the targets (i.e., spurious correlation). In this paper, we propose to equip attention mechanism with causal inference, which is a powerful tool to identify the real causal effects. Our model is based on the potential outcome framework, where the item features are regarded as the treatment and the outcome is the predicted user preference. In specific, the real causal relation of each feature on the outcome is measured by the individual treatment effect (ITE). In order to distill the causal information into the attention learning process, we minimize the distance between the traditional attention weights and the normalized ITE. With such causal regularization, the learned attention weights can reflect the real causal effects, which are expected to correct the feature importances for improved recommendation performance. We conduct extensive experiments based on three real-world datasets to demonstrate our model's effectiveness.

Contributions

  • We try to arouse people's attention on the potential spurious correlations captured by existing attention-based recommender models.
  • We design an ITE enhanced attention mechanism to identify the real feature-target relations, which can better discriminate feature importances and lead to improved recommendation performance.
  • Extensive experiments based on three real-world datasets are conducted to demonstrate our model's effectiveness.
